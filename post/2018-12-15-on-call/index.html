    On-Call

    @ · Dec 15, 2018 · 3 min read

    I really liked Henrik’s blog post about on-call. Many of the points ring true with my experience over the years.

    I’ve learnt something about scheduling on-call shifts over the past 18 months which leads to meaningful improvements work-life balance, and wanted to pass the idea on.

    Long rotations are filled with compromises:

    Compared to smaller time slices, trading a week is inherently harder. Because it’s larger amount of time, others are more likely to have conflicts with the week you’re trying to get out of. Sometimes I’ll want to go out with friends for someone’s birthday, but not on-call. Longer rotations mean that I’m stuck asking to reschedule an entire week later, rather than just a few days.

    Any handoff time overlaps with vacation planning: Wednesday means you can’t take either week off. Friday (or Monday) makes you trade the entire week just for a weekend.

    A different schedule:

    Where I work, many teams have been following this schedule for a little over a year:

    • A weekday schedule: Monday at noon until Friday at noon
    • A weekend schedule: Friday at noon until Monday at noon

    The same people are on both rotations, but it’s configured not to schedule consecutive weekend-then-weekday shifts.

    Sun Mon Tue Wed Thu Fri Sat
                 
                 
                 
                 
    ...

    As an example: I was on-call last weekend. In a few weeks I’m on for a weekday shift.

    We’ve found this schedule to be an improvement because:

    • If you’re out of town for the weekend, switching on Monday at Noon allows you to catch an early Monday morning flight with a large margin of error.
    • The same exists for Friday afternoon: you can make plans that necessitate leaving early in the day
    • Some coworkers with children prefer extra time during weekdays, others prefer going out to party on weekends. With a diverse team, these two kinds of on-call shift can be traded to simplify work-life balance

    Setting this up:

    If interested, here’s how to configure PagerDuty to make this happen:

    • Look up the order of your on-call schedule (a, b, c, d, e, f, a …)
    • Cycle it off by half a phase, we’ll use this later (d, e, f, a, b, c, d…)
    • Edit the on-call schedule to create a second layer for weekends:
      • Restrict that layer to Fri @ noon - Mon @ noon
      • Add users from the order in the second step
      • Make sure the effective-on date of the second layer and the first layer are the same

    It would be great if Pagerduty had configuration recipes to manage nuanced schedule layering like this.

    Should you try this out, please let me know how it goes.
